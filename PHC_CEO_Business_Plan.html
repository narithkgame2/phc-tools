Advisory Flow — 5 Stages
Stage 1 · First Contact
Qualify & Understand
  • Respond within 2 hours via preferred channel
  • Ask 3 qualifying questions: budget, timeline, purpose
  • Send lead magnet PDF immediately
  • Set clear next step: "2–3 matching options within 24 hours"
Stage 2 · Proposal
Tailored Options (Max 3)
  • Never send more than 3 — decision fatigue kills deals
  • Use PHC Proposal template — branded, data-driven
  • Include net yield, title type, foreign ownership eligibility
  • Always state a recommendation and why
Stage 3 · Site Visit
Experience the Project
  • Arrange within 1 week of proposal
  • Brief client before: what they'll see, what to ask
  • Never pressure during visit
  • Written debrief within 24 hours
Stage 4 · Decision Support
Remove Blockers
  • Ask what would need to be true to feel confident
  • Provide legal due diligence: title verification, SPA review
  • Connect with developer finance team for payment options
  • Japanese: full written risk summary, never verbal-only
Stage 5 · Close & Onboard
Sign & Stay Connected
  • Accompany client to signing — never send alone
  • Written summary of all commitments made
  • 30-day check-in after signing — builds referral relationship
  • Bring into property management conversation if relevant

90-Day Sprint
REFRESHED JUL 2026
Superseded the original Q2 sprint, which assumed a pre-website, pre-bot, zero-ad-spend starting point. This version starts from where PHC actually is: infrastructure built, outreach drafted, nothing sent yet.
90-Day Objective
Send what's built. Prove the message. Then scale it.
The bottleneck isn't infrastructure anymore — it's execution. Nothing new gets built until the referral and outbound work already sitting finished has gone out.
Days 1–7 · Week 1
Send the Referral Outreach
  • Send the already-drafted referral message to past clients (15% of commission, finalized weeks ago)
  • Log every "how did you hear about us" response in the Referred By column — the only tracking mechanism, keep it disciplined from day 1
  • Post 1–2 of the 5 drafted outbound community templates to gauge response before posting the rest
Days 8–14 · Week 2
Fix the Content Mix
  • Shift the 4 pilot Telegram channels toward the 70/30 educational-first mix — still nearly all listings right now
  • Post remaining outbound templates (JP/DE-market, forum variants) — JP copy needs real translation/localization first
  • Load real client data into the Payment Reminder Bot (channel + groupChatId) — tested but not doing real work yet
Days 15–30 · Weeks 3–4
Read the Response
  • Track referral response rate and outbound engagement — this is the signal that decides Month 2, not a guess
  • Grow pilot channel subscribers past the current ~0–4 each — no distribution advantage without an audience
  • Goal: enough response data to know which message/segment is working, before spending a dollar on ads
Days 31–45 · Weeks 5–6
Double Down on What Responded
  • Whichever channel/segment produced real replies — put more into that, not evenly across all
  • Add LinkedIn presence — still the missing platform for the DE/European segment
  • Keep the referral loop running: every closed deal triggers the next referral ask
Days 46–60 · Weeks 7–8
Decide on Paid Ads
  • Only now — with real message/audience data — decide whether paid ads are worth testing, and on which message
  • If outbound + referral alone produce enough pipeline, paid ads may stay deferred longer. That's a legitimate outcome.
Days 61–90 · Weeks 9–12
Reassess the Whole Roadmap
  • Revisit Progress Dashboard — how much moved from "planned" to "live"
  • Re-run the Mission Control snapshot with real current revenue/lead numbers instead of placeholders
  • Decide whether agent-hire trigger conditions are met based on actual deal flow

Risk Register
Q2 DRAFT
The threats most likely to derail PHC's $250K target — and the mitigation for each.
Risk Severity Matrix
HIGH
Lead engine fails to generate consistent flow
If 30 days of paid ads produce fewer than 5 qualified leads, the quarterly deal target can't be met.
Mitigation: run ads 2 weeks, review CPL. Above $50/lead → test new creative/targeting. Keep 3 alternative channels ready.
HIGH
Team bandwidth collapse (3 people, 5 segments)
Nick, Monika, and Reza covering sales, rental management, advisory, content, systems, and BD simultaneously — not sustainable at scale.
Mitigation: one primary segment per person. Nick=JP+outreach, Monika=Khmer+Overseas KH, Reza=DE+European. No cross-segment handling without consultation.
MED
Developer concentration risk (Megakim + Tanichu)
If either developer delays, halts sales, or cuts commission, PHC's inventory position weakens — 2 developers isn't enough diversification for $250K.
Mitigation: begin relationship with 1–2 additional developers by Q2 (Le Condé/Wangfu, Peninsula, G.A.T.O/MIRAKU are candidates).
MED
CGT uncertainty slows buyer decisions
Capital Gains Tax (effective Jan 2026) has shifted buyer psychology away from short-term flips.
Mitigation: lead all investor conversations with long-hold framing — PHC's advisory stance is already CGT-appropriate. Make it a differentiator.
MED
Brand credibility gap — unverifiable track record claims
"500+ clients, 1,200+ transactions, 98% satisfaction" stated without evidence. A sophisticated JP/DE investor will ask for proof.
Mitigation: remove until evidenced. Replace with verifiable specifics: "3 completed Megakim projects · freehold strata expertise · 15 years combined experience."
LOW
Cambodia political / regulatory risk
Foreign ownership laws, strata regulations, or repatriation rules could change. Unlikely short-term but always present in a developing market.
Mitigation: stay current with REAKH legal updates. State "regulations subject to change" in proposals. Never guarantee legal outcomes.
