Advisory Flow — 5 Stages
Stage 1 · First Contact
Qualify & Understand
  • Respond within 2 hours via preferred channel (WhatsApp, LINE, email)
  • Ask 3 qualifying questions: budget, timeline, purpose
  • Send lead magnet PDF immediately — shows value before asking for anything
  • Set clear next step: "I will send you 2–3 options that match your profile within 24 hours"
Stage 2 · Proposal
Tailored Options (Max 3)
  • Never send more than 3 options — decision fatigue kills deals
  • Use PHC Proposal template (docx) — branded, professional, data-driven
  • Include net yield (not gross), title type, foreign ownership eligibility
  • Always state: "Option 2 is what I would recommend for your profile, and here is why"
Stage 3 · Site Visit
Experience the Project
  • Arrange showroom or site visit within 1 week of proposal
  • Brief client before visit: what they will see, what questions to ask
  • Never pressure during visit — let the project speak
  • Send written debrief within 24 hours: what you saw, next steps, any questions answered
Stage 4 · Decision Support
Remove Blockers
  • Ask: "What would need to be true for you to feel confident?" — surface real objections
  • Provide legal due diligence: title verification, SPA review summary
  • Connect with developer finance team for payment plan options
  • For Japanese: send full written risk summary. Never verbal only.
Stage 5 · Close & Onboard
Sign & Stay Connected
  • Accompany client to signing — never send them alone
  • Send written summary of all commitments made (payment schedule, handover date)
  • 30-day check-in after signing — builds referral relationship
  • Add to property management conversation if relevant

Risk Register
The threats most likely to derail PHC's $250K target — and the mitigation for each.
Risk Severity Matrix
HIGH
Lead engine fails to generate consistent flow
If 30 days of paid ads produce fewer than 5 qualified leads, the quarterly deal target cannot be met. This is the highest probability risk given 90 days at 0 producing only 2 leads.
Mitigation: Run ads for 2 weeks then review CPL (cost per lead). If above $50/lead, test new creative or targeting. Have 3 alternative channels ready (community outreach, YouTube, Reza's LinkedIn).
HIGH
Team bandwidth collapse (3 people, 5 segments)
Nick, Monika, and Reza are covering development sales, rental management, client advisory, content creation, system building, and business development simultaneously. This is not sustainable at scale.
Mitigation: Assign one primary segment per person. Nick = Japanese + Japanese outreach. Monika = Khmer + Overseas Cambodian. Reza = German + European. No cross-segment handling without consultation.
MED
Developer concentration risk (Megakim + Tanichu)
If either developer delays a project, halts sales, or reduces commission rates, PHC's inventory position weakens significantly. Two developers is not enough diversification for a $250K revenue target.
Mitigation: Begin relationship with 1–2 additional developers by Q2. Le Condé (Wangfu), Peninsula (separate developer), and G.A.T.O Tower (MIRAKU) are natural candidates. Do not resell without proper due diligence.
MED
CGT uncertainty slows buyer decisions
Capital Gains Tax effective January 2026 has shifted buyer psychology away from short-term flips. Some buyers are waiting to see how enforcement plays out before committing.
Mitigation: Lead all investor conversations with a long-hold framing. PHC's advisory stance (hold 5+ years, net yield focus, rental strategy) is already CGT-appropriate. Make CGT clarity a PHC differentiator.
MED
Brand credibility gap — unverifiable track record claims
"500+ clients, 1,200+ transactions, 98% satisfaction" are stated without evidence. A sophisticated Japanese or German investor will ask for proof. If PHC cannot provide case studies, testimonials, or transaction data, these claims become a liability.
Mitigation: Remove these claims from all materials until they can be evidenced. Replace with specific, verifiable data points: "3 completed Megakim projects · Freehold strata title expertise · 15 years combined experience."
LOW
Cambodia political / regulatory risk
Foreign ownership laws, strata title regulations, or repatriation rules could change. Unlikely in the short term but always present in a developing market.
Mitigation: Stay current with REAKH legal updates. Include "regulations subject to change" in all proposals. Never guarantee legal outcomes — recommend clients consult a local lawyer.
