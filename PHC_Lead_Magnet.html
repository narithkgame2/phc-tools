Why investors choose Cambodia
  • 100% foreign ownership via strata title — legally clean, LMAP-registered in your name
  • USD-denominated — no currency conversion risk on price, rent, or resale
  • Gross rental yields 7–8% in BKK1; net 4.5–6% after fees and taxes
  • No capital controls — repatriate rental income and sale proceeds freely
  • Lower entry than Bangkok, HCMC, or Singapore for comparable yield profile
  • Growing expat community and regional professional tenant base in BKK1
Key risks — understand before you buy
  • ~72,000 condo units with ~15% vacancy — research project-level occupancy, not city averages
  • Developer delivery risk on off-plan — verify track record and escrow terms before signing
  • Luxury segment vacancy hit 42% in 2024 — mid-range near local demand outperforms
  • Property management quality varies widely — net yield is the only honest number
  • CGT (20% on gains) in effect since January 2026 — always factor into exit modelling
  • Exit liquidity is lower than developed markets — plan for 3–6 months to resale

Tax & Legal Framework PHC · INVESTMENT GUIDE 2026
Compliance Essentials
Know Before You Sign
Cambodia's tax regime is relatively straightforward — but agents who do not disclose CGT and rental tax upfront lose client trust permanently.
Property Tax Summary · Cambodia 2026
Transfer Tax
4%
One-time on purchase. On $200K property: $8,000. Exemptions extended to end 2026 — full exemption under $70K, preferential rate under $210K.
Annual Property Tax
0.1%
On assessed value above $25,000 threshold. Most condos under $200K result in minimal annual liability. Paid to Phnom Penh Capital Hall.
Rental Income Tax
10% / 14%
10% for Cambodian residents · 14% for foreign non-residents. On $12,000/yr rental: Cambodian pays $1,200, foreigner pays $1,680. Always factor into net yield calculations for foreign investors.
Capital Gains Tax (CGT)
20%
In effect since January 2026. On taxable gain = sale minus (purchase + documented costs). Alternative: 80% flat deduction method. Example: Buy $100K, sell $200K, costs $40K → taxable $60K × 20% = $12,000 CGT. Always disclose. Always model into exit scenarios.
Foreign Ownership Rules · Non-Negotiable
1
Above ground floor only
Foreigners may only legally own units on the 1st floor and above. Ground floor is prohibited regardless of title type.
2
70% foreign quota per building
Max 70% of all units in any single building may be foreign-held. Always verify remaining quota before recommending a project.
3
Strata title (LMAP) is mandatory for foreign freehold
Only strata title confers legal foreign ownership. Soft title is for Cambodian nationals only. Verify title type per unit — not per building.
4
Land: Cambodian Trust is the compliant structure — not a nominee
For land or non-strata assets, a Cambodian Trust under the Law on Trust 2019 is the correct structure. This is formally registered and legally enforceable. Informal nominee arrangements are illegal and PHC does not support them.

The Buying Process PHC · INVESTMENT GUIDE 2026
Step by Step
From Enquiry to Title Registration
A straightforward 7-step process. PHC accompanies you at every stage — from unit selection to legal review to title registration in your name.
1
Unit Selection & Booking
$500–$2,000 booking fee. Confirm refund terms in writing before paying.
2
SPA Review & Sign
Legal review required. Check price, handover date, penalties, and management terms.
3
Downpayment
20–30% due after SPA signing. Wire transfer or local bank.
4
Payment Schedule
Monthly, quarterly, or milestone-based. Match schedule to your cash flow.
5
Handover & Inspection
Final walk-through. Document all defects in a written snag list before signing acceptance.
6
Final Payment
Balance due on handover. Settle via bank transfer or approved financing.
7
Title Registration
Strata title registered with MLMUPC in your name. PHC assists with full documentation.
