Property Tax Summary — Cambodia 2026 · Source: REAKH Report
Annual Property Tax (TOIP)
0.1% / yr
Applies to value above ~$25,000. On a $100K property: taxable $80K minus $25K = $55K × 0.1% = $55/year.
Very low annual holding cost. Not a deterrent for investors.
Transfer Tax (Stamp Duty)
4% of value
One-time tax on ownership transfer. On $200K: $8,000. Exemptions extended until end of 2026 for properties under $70K (full exemption) and under $210K (preferential).
Capital Gains Tax (CGT)
20% on profit
Applied to the taxable gain. Gain = sale price minus (purchase + documented costs). Example: Buy $100K, sell $200K, costs $40K → taxable $60K × 20% = $12,000 CGT. Alternative: 80% flat deduction method.
Effective January 2026. Always disclose. Always factor into exit scenarios.
Rental Income Tax
10% / 14%
10% for Cambodian residents · 14% for foreign non-residents. On $12,000/yr rental: Cambodian pays $1,200 · Foreigner pays $1,680.
Always factor into net yield calculations for foreign investors.
Foreign Ownership Rules — Non-Negotiable
1
Above ground floor only
Foreigners can only legally own units on the 1st floor and above. Ground floor is prohibited regardless of title type. Never show ground-floor units to foreign buyers.
2
70% foreign quota per building
Maximum 70% of all units in any single building can be held by foreign nationals. Always verify remaining foreign quota before recommending. Flag if under 5% remains.
3
Strata title is mandatory for foreign ownership
Only strata title (freehold) is valid for foreign condo ownership. Soft title is for Cambodian nationals only. All PHC-recommended projects carry strata title — verify per unit.
4
No informal nominee structures
Using a Cambodian citizen to hold property on behalf of a foreigner with only a verbal or side agreement is illegal, legally unenforceable, and not supported by PHC. Redirect all informal nominee enquiries to compliant options.
5
Cambodian Trust = compliant alternative for land & non-condo assets
The Law on Trust 2019 allows a licensed trustee to legally hold land or non-strata assets on behalf of a foreign beneficiary. This is NOT a nominee structure — it is formally registered and legally enforceable. Used for: land banking, villa purchases, and airport corridor plays. Always require a licensed trustee firm with written trust deed. Reference: 552 of 683 registered trusts in 2023 were foreign-held.

Techo International Airport — Investment Corridor Guide
✓ Status: Operational since September 2025
Techo International Airport (Kandal Province) opened September 2025, replacing Phnom Penh International for most commercial traffic. Located ~30km south of BKK1 along Hun Sen Boulevard. Designed capacity: 13M passengers/year (Phase 1), scalable to 50M.
1
Corridor play: Hun Sen Boulevard
Land values along the airport corridor (Kandal Province, Chbar Ampov fringe) are showing fresh price momentum. Best suited for 5–8 year land banking. Must hold via Cambodian Trust with a verified trustee firm — not verbal agreements.
2
Adjacent suburbs: early signals
Chak Angre, Prek Pnov, and southern Phnom Penh districts are seeing early spillover. Industrial and logistics land near the access roads is most active. Residential still lagging — airport-effect pricing typically takes 3–5 years post-opening.
3
Who this suits
High-net-worth clients ($300K+) with a speculative allocation, long time horizon, and no need for rental income. Not suitable for yield-seeking investors or anyone needing liquidity within 5 years.
4
What to watch
New road and BRT infrastructure commitments from government — these directly unlock land value. Also watch airline route announcements: each new long-haul route increases corridor premium. CBRE and REAKH publish quarterly corridor updates.
⚠ Do not over-promise on airport-linked land
Liquidity is near-zero on undeveloped corridor land. Resale requires finding another long-term buyer. Zoning can change. Present as a speculative allocation — never as a primary investment strategy for first-time investors.
Critical Reminders for Every Client Conversation
⚠ Never say this
"Guaranteed returns" · "This will definitely go up" · "Don't worry about CGT" · "Only X units left". These destroy trust permanently with serious investors.
✓ Always disclose before showing any project
Exit liquidity risk · Net yield (not gross) · CGT on exit · Developer delivery track record · Management quality · Foreign quota remaining
✓ Gross vs Net — always clarify
Gross yield = rental income ÷ purchase price. Net yield = rental income after management fees, vacancy, and taxes ÷ purchase price. Average difference: management fee ($1.8/sqm) + ~10% vacancy + 14% rental tax for foreigners. Never quote gross without clarifying.
